Super Bowl History

The Super Bowl was the result of the fierce rivalry between two competing football leagues - the AFL (American Football League) and the NFL (National Football League) In June 1966, an NFL-AFL merger was announced. The merger called for a common draft of college players and a championship game—the Super Bowl—to start in 1967. The new league would be called the National Football League with the previous rivals split into two divisions: the National Football Conference (NFC) and the American Football Conference (AFC).
